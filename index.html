Origami

Origami is the art of paper folding, which is often associated with Japanese culture. In modern usage, the word "origami" is used as an inclusive term for all folding practices, regardless of their culture of origin

Some facts about camel

  1. There are over 160 words for camel in Arabic alone.
  2. Camels are very strong and can carry 900 pounds
  3. Camels can completely shut their nose during sandstorms.
  4. Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour

Some facts about pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals
  2. They will be seen in flock of 20-30 birds
  3. Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
  4. Pigeons tend to raise two chicks at the same time.

Some facts about bear

  1. Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals.
  2. Bears grieve deeply for others.
  3. Bears have excellent senses of smell, sight and hearing.
  4. Asiatic bear build nests in the trees.

Some facts about chameleon

  1. THEIR FEET WORK LIKE SALAD TONGS
  2. ALMOST HALF OF ALL KNOWN SPECIES LIVE IN MADAGASCAR.
  3. CHAMELEONS VARY WILDLY IN TERMS OF SIZE.
  4. UNLIKE MANY LIZARDS, CHAMELEONS CAN'T REGROW THEIR TAILS.

Some facts about panda

  1. Giant pandas are good at climbing trees
  2. They can also swim
  3. Pandas are "lazy" — eating and sleeping make their day.
  4. An adult can eat 12–38 kilos of bamboo per day.

Some facts about Flying cicada

  1. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies
  2. They're true bugs
  3. Most have red-orange eyes.
  4. Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.
